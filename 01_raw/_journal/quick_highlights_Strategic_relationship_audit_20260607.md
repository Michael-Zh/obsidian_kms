Jeroen (ISFJ, autistic tendencies, raised in Dutch context) has almost certainly never interrogated his monogamy. The disclosure conversation can be framed as inviting him into that inquiry — not asking him to go open, but asking him what he *actually* wants. Less threatening entry point.

Worth clarifying internally: is the need for outside encounters primarily *physical touch deficit* (somatic need not met at home) or *novelty/BDSM/curiosity*? Different answers = different SLA designs.
- **The "collector drive" question reframed**: The video's evolutionary biology framing (some people are natural "top shaggers," others natural "nesters," and the mix serves the species) provides a non-pathologizing lens for your Q1 research question. It normalizes variation without requiring you to justify your drive. Useful framing for the disclosure conversation.
