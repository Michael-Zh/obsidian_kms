## 第一步：给财务状况做一次 X 光检查

很多人并不知道自己的净资产到底是多少。原因不是数字太复杂，而是直面这些数字会让人不舒服。于是钱进来又出去，没有形成积累，也没有产生复利。这不是单纯的金钱问题，而是系统问题。

Most people have no idea what their net worth actually is. Honestly, it is not because the numbers are complicated. It is because looking feels uncomfortable, so they just do not. They are not broke; they make decent money. But it goes in and it goes out. Nothing compounds. That is not a money problem. That is a system problem.

富有的人通常会付费请别人帮助他们面对数字、整理数字，因为清晰地知道自己站在哪里，是一切改变的起点。真正的修复方式，不是立刻寻找更好的股票，而是先看见完整的财务图景。

What wealthy people do differently is that they pay someone to force them to look at the numbers and lay everything out. Clarity—knowing exactly where you stand—is where everything starts. That is the fix: not a better stock pick, but finally seeing the full picture.

孙子强调“知己”非常重要，但很多人在金钱问题上完全跳过了这一步。他们直接问“我应该买什么”，却从未审视自己已经在做什么。这也是很多人投资失败的根源。

Sun Tzu wrote about knowing yourself before anything else. Most people skip this completely with money. They go straight to asking, “What should I buy?” without ever looking at what they are already doing. Honestly, that is exactly why most of them lose.

## 第二步：把“想变富”变成一个明确数字

“我想变富”不是目标，而是一种感觉。大脑很难围绕一种模糊感觉持续行动；但如果给它一个具体、可达、可推进的数字，行动就会变得更清楚。每达成一个里程碑，大脑都会获得反馈，并继续积累动力。

“I want to be rich” is not a goal; it is a feeling. Your brain cannot work on a feeling. Give it a real number, something concrete, reachable, and something that shifts, and everything changes. This is not motivational talk; it is how dopamine works. You hit a milestone, you get a hit, and then you build momentum.

真正擅长理财的人未必更自律，他们只是更懂得利用大脑的反馈机制。设定目标时，不要只被社会推着追求更高收入、更大数字、永远不够。更好的问题是：你真正需要多少钱，才能过上对你而言有意义的生活？这个数字才是你的目标。

People who are genuinely good with money understand how to use dopamine. They are not necessarily more disciplined; they just know how to use their brain. Most people get this wrong. Society tells us to always want more income, bigger numbers, and never enough. A better question is: how much do you actually need to live a life that feels meaningful to you? That is your target.

过去，要算清这个目标可能需要花很多钱和时间请顾问。现在，你可以告诉 Claude：你希望什么时候退休、希望每月获得多少被动收入，并让它为你生成三条路径：保守型、稳健型和进取型。每条路径都应包含每月储蓄额、诚实的取舍、缺口、时间线和所需收益率。

It used to cost a lot of money and time with an advisor to figure this out. Now you can tell Claude when you want to retire, how much monthly passive income you want, and ask it to build three paths to get there: conservative, moderate, and aggressive, with exact monthly savings and honest trade-offs for each. It comes back with your gap, your timeline, and your required return.

更进一步，Claude 还可以在不同经济情景下运行测算，让你不是只为单一版本的未来做计划。一旦你拥有这个数字，投资就不再主要依赖情绪。每个决策都可以简化为一个问题：它让我离目标更近，还是更远？

It can also run the numbers under different economic scenarios, so you are not just planning for one version of the future. Once you have that number, investing stops being emotional. Every decision becomes simple: does this move me closer to or further away from my target? That is it—not philosophy, just a system to follow.

By the time you finish your research, someone may already have bought it cheaper. Earnings reports, ETF screening, macro trends—these are days of work, and the market does not pause and wait for you to catch up. Professional investors know this, so they do not do all of it themselves. They hire teams of analysts whose whole job is to move fast and do the analysis.

With Claude Financial Plugin, you can do something similar. Set up multiple agents running in parallel, scanning the sectors you care about on whatever schedule you want: hourly, daily, or weekly. You are not guessing, because it connects directly to real data from providers like Morningstar.

Trigger sector analysis and you get the macro picture in seconds. Run comps analysis and you see exactly how a company stacks up against its rivals. Days of work can be done in minutes. Your ideas probably are not the problem; your timing is. This is how you fix it.

很多人两年前建立了一个投资组合，此后几乎没有再调整。他们称之为“买入并持有”，但有时这只是回避的另一种说法。复盘投资组合不是额外工作，而是策略本身的一部分。

It is typical to build a portfolio two years ago and not touch it since. You call it buy and hold, but honestly, it can just be avoidance with a better name. Reviewing your portfolio is not extra work; it is part of the strategy.

多数人之所以跳过复盘，是因为坐下来读一份 40 页的财报确实很累。每个人都有工作和生活，很少有人有充足时间做这些事，而金融行业也在某种程度上依赖这种信息不对称。

The reason most people skip it is that sitting down with a 40-page earnings report for hours is genuinely exhausting. We all have jobs and lives. Nobody has enough time for that, and the financial industry kind of counts on it.

AI 改变了这件事。你可以在 Claude 中运行财报分析，让它从报告中提取真正的风险信号；再把自己的投资组合粘贴进再平衡流程，让它展示你的风险结构如何变化，并一步步说明你应该如何处理。

Here is what AI changes. Run earnings analysis in Claude and it pulls out the actual red flags from the report. Then paste your portfolio into a rebalance workflow. It shows you exactly how your risk has shifted and walks you through what you need to do about it.

整个过程可能只需要五分钟。真正创造长期回报的人，不一定更聪明，而是更频繁地复盘并调整策略。AI 让这件事对普通人也变得容易。

Five minutes—that is the whole session. The people who build real returns are not necessarily smarter. They just review and adjust their strategy very often. AI has made that easy for everyone.
