## Meeting Transcript Analysis — J + Vivi Communication

*Added in same session based on transcripts from 2026-05-29, 2026-06-01, and 2026-06-05.*

### The core structural mismatch

J operates in BLUF mode. Michael operates in context-first mode. This produces a consistent meeting pattern: Michael front-loads background J already knows, J extracts the hypothesis through questions, J ends up supplying the confidence framing and next steps that Michael should have brought in. This happens in all three transcripts.

### Pattern 1 — Background before hypothesis

Michael leads with context (the BA strict airline fact, the H2 2025 comparison rationale, the algorithm timeline) before stating what he believes. J then has to interrupt with questions to get to the finding. In May 29, Michael speaks for ~90 seconds of setup before J's first question. The fix is structural: open every J meeting with one sentence hypothesis, then bring the data as support.

> **New structure:** "I believe X because Y. Here's the data. My assumption is Z — if that's wrong, it breaks because [reason]. My proposed next step is [action]."

### Pattern 2 — Uncertainty stated mid-stream instead of as named caveat

"I'm not quite sure if we can confidently say..." (May 29) comes *while Michael is presenting data* — it reads as self-undermining rather than methodological rigour. J then supplies the confidence framing Michael should have brought: *"We can say we assume sourcing from BA is not an issue because BA is strict airline."* The analytical caution is correct; the delivery converts it into a liability.

> **Fix:** "My assumption here is X — you can see it holds in [data]. If that assumption is wrong, it breaks because [reason]." Same caution, different framing.

### Pattern 3 — No proposed next step; waiting for J to close

Both May 29 and June 5 end with J explicitly assigning Michael's next steps. The strategist move is to arrive with a proposed action and let J approve or redirect. June 5 ends with J saying "Look for the upsell ratio tag for EY" — Michael had the data; he should have proposed that step himself.

### Pattern 4 — Doing data infrastructure work instead of delegating

J said "That's their job. Involve Lily and Kelly" in May 29. June 5 still has 15 minutes of Hive→BigQuery self-service discussion — Michael navigating tutorial pages, permission approvals, three separate people. J redirected again: "Let me talk to Lily and Harry." The pattern: Michael hits a blocker, goes down the rabbit hole alone, then reports it to J. The patch: first question when hitting data access is "Who owns this?" — then one message to Lily, not three weeks of self-service.

### Pattern 5 — Not accepting simplifications (Vivi meeting)

Vivi offered a clean frame mid-June 1 meeting: "Coverage issue OR ranking issue — those are the only two causes." Michael re-added complexity: "but also fare family design, and price..." Vivi's simplification was a gift. The right move is to take it and work inside the frame: "Yes — let me tell you where we are on coverage." Accepting the simplification is not dumbing down; it's operating at Vivi's speed.
