## O1: Transform raw IBU pain points into clear, quantified definitions for FBU

The majority of issues in H1 came from proactive discovery through the 40-airline brand fare audit rather than inbound regional requests. I triaged findings by strategic focus and business value, responded to regional requests with pre-work and domain knowledge, and formalized the analytical framework — supply chain diagnostic funnel from supply to display — giving the team a shared structure for root cause classification and prioritization. On the void/24h finding, J drove the initial discovery; I supported the data synthesis and deep-dives, then drove cross-BU resolution with FBU — surfacing a blind spot they had no prior visibility on, confirmed and roadmapped as an H2 initiative.

Growth edge: identifying obvious performance gaps and needle movers — two strategist standards I've internalized over H1 and am actively building into my analytical habit for H2.

---

## O2: Ensure all stakeholders stay aligned and all shared data is accurate

Through regular FBU stand-ups, I built a close working relationship with Vivi — raising issues early and maintaining shared alignment. IBU communication evolved from bi-weekly emails into a layered framework: Lark notifications, emails, and live syncs. I built the project hub as a single source of truth, solving a centralization gap from the project's start. J and Serena explicitly recognized my ability to bring clarity to a complex, chaotic project through structured documentation and clean visual formats.

Gaps: tracking updates fell behind during heavy analytical periods; front-end FBU information was delayed due to their internal transitions; data quality self-checks were not without exception. Higher standard in H2, alongside more proactive regional team engagement.

---

## O3: Monitor the project closely to identify and solve problems before they impact the timeline

The formal health check cadence was consciously deprioritized in alignment with J, as capacity was concentrated on audit framework, communication infrastructure, and issue quantification. Instead: we launched a Brand Fare Audit framework with regional teams, and I built a coverage scraper prototype independently — FBU assessed it as covering 80–90% of their requirements. Scalability remains to be validated. On Growth Finder: the void/24h finding is the H1 example of identifying a significant business risk. Quick wins remain an open area for H2.

---

## O4: Effectively manage extra requests from IBU and FBU to keep global projects moving

Ad-hoc requests were limited in volume this half-year, with most emerging directly from the brand fare audit or within the upsell scope (such as the recent FD case). Looking ahead, I want to be more proactive in H2 about engaging regional teams — not just managing their requests, but actively involving them in audit coverage where their local market knowledge would accelerate progress.

## Overall Summary

H1 was a year of building from scratch — new role, new industry, project still finding its shape. Within six months: a structured audit framework, a centralized project hub, a layered cross-BU communication system, and a quantified finding — the void/24h display override — confirmed by FBU and entered into H2 roadmap. J and Serena explicitly recognized the ability to bring structure and clarity to a chaotic, multi-stakeholder project.

Growth edge: identify opportunities with clear gap in metric and developing the needle mover instinct — both internalized over H1 and the primary focus for H2. The project does not yet have a visible business result, but the analytical and organizational groundwork that makes it possible has been laid.
