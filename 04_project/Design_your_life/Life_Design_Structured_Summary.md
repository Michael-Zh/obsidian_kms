## Step 2: Building a Compass — Workview & Lifeview

DYL asks you to articulate *why* you work and *what the world is for* — not as abstract philosophy, but as lived operating principles.

### Workview
*What is work for? What makes it good?*

Synthesized from session:
- Work should provide **meaning**, not just income — but meaning comes from the *architecture* of the work (finding patterns, building frameworks, enabling others), not from job titles
- Ideal work rhythm: **~50% pattern-finding / ~25% coaching/teaching / ~25% execution** — roughly 3 days/week on creative or somatic work, 2 days on analytical
- Work should allow **autonomy over time, location, and pace**
- The flight upsell project — specifically the communication framework built — was a high-achievement signal: systems-building that enables others

### Lifeview
*What is life for? What gives it meaning?*

Synthesized from session:
- Finding patterns reduces cognitive load — *"I don't want to memorize the world, I want to derive it"*
- The world is real and interest-based; **rational trade-offs and calculated compromise** are acceptable
- Bringing **positive energy** to others matters; not wanting to impose views, but to enable capability
- Aesthetic experience — bodies, movement, beauty — is not peripheral; it's a core source of meaning

**Core compass label (from session):** "赋能架构师" — *Empowerment Architect*. The architecture side builds systems; the empowerment side enables others to thrive inside them.

## Step 3: Wayfinding — Good Time Journal Patterns

DYL uses a Good Time Journal (engagement + energy log) to surface what activities produce flow. The journal was completed in the Gemini session (V2 doc) with the following outputs:

**High engagement / high energy (flow states):**
- **Imposing order on chaos** — building clear, structured, aesthetically satisfying communication frameworks from messy inputs (e.g., the flight upsell communication system)
- **Translating complex knowledge to empower others** — writing clear tutorials, delivering high-quality presentations; the act of making something hard accessible

**Low engagement / energy drain (AEIOU blind spots):**
- Navigating large volumes of ambiguous metrics and data without clear direction — the feeling of lacking intuition or sensitivity; inability to decisively cut
- Bureaucratic process for its own sake — administrative paperwork with no strategic purpose

**Flow pattern:** The throughline is *translation* — taking something complex or chaotic and rendering it as clear, structured, transmittable. This appears in data work (chaos → insight → framework), movement work (raw sensation → teachable system), and knowledge management (scattered inputs → synthesis). It's not creation from nothing; it's *curating order from noise*.

**The critical asymmetry:** High-energy states are about enabling *others* to understand. Low-energy states are about navigating ambiguity *alone* without clear signal. This suggests the work environment design matters as much as the work itself: Michael needs a context with clear feedback loops and a clear role as translator/synthesizer, not a context that requires pure intuition-based judgment.

## Step 4: Getting Unstuck — Identifying Gravity Problems

DYL distinguishes *anchor problems* (can be solved) from *gravity problems* (feel like constraints but are actually assumptions). From the session:

**Gravity problem 1:** *"I need to wait until finances are sorted before exploring alternative careers."*  
**Reframe:** The "boring investor" 5% rule. You don't need to overhaul your life — invest 5% of monthly time into a new skill. The Iyengar workshop is not a 3-year commitment; it's a low-cost prototype that tests whether you actually want to go deeper.

**Gravity problem 2:** *"I'm not creative enough to be a curator/educator."*  
**Reframe:** Curation, synthesis, and growing seeds into trees are high-order creative acts. Creation ≠ generating from nothing. The 6-year arc from zero to teaching is direct evidence of this capacity.

**Gravity problem 3:** *"I must evaluate every data assumption and life option perfectly before choosing."*  
**Reframe:** In complex environments, there are no perfect options — only selected prototypes. The goal is to set artificial constraints, filter quickly, and test. Progressive fidelity beats perfect analysis.

**Gravity problem 4:** *"Body/movement work must generate income through time-for-money exchange."*  
**Reframe (from V2):** The analytical and systems-building capability can create passive income leverage in the movement space — methodologies, experience frameworks, content. The constraint is not body/income incompatibility; it's defaulting to the wrong income model.

## Step 5: Designing Your Lives — Three Odyssey Plans

DYL asks you to sketch three distinct 5-year plans. Each has a title, a 6-word headline, a confidence bar, and a gauge for resources, engagement, and coherence.

### Plan 1: Corporate Strategist (The Current Path, Evolved)
**Headline:** Become the strategist who builds judgment, not just frameworks  
**5-Year Arc:** Current analyst role → senior strategist with autonomous scope → advisor/consultant track with portfolio flexibility  
**Confidence:** High (known territory; proven capability)  
**What makes it work:** The manager-as-target-model reframe — reverse-engineer her strategic intuition rather than waiting for it to emerge. Build a personal "commercial judgment principles" knowledge base.  
**What's missing:** Doesn't solve the energy problem on its own. Work may remain functional-but-not-alive unless the autonomy and movement dimensions are integrated.

### Plan 2: Movement Educator (The Realistic Pivot)
**Headline:** Build a somatic practice portfolio before financial independence  
**5-Year Arc:** Prototype experiments (Iyengar workshop, Studio Anna Mora 1-on-1) → accumulate certifications selectively (Gyrotonic, Pilates, targeted Iyengar) → part-time teaching alongside full-time work → independent practice  
**Confidence:** Medium (genuine interest confirmed; financial runway unclear)  
**What makes it work:** Integrates the body/movement domain that already takes 5 days/week, so it's not adding to the schedule — it's converting existing practice into professional capability  
**The ladder:** Plan 2 is the realistic stairs to Plan 3. Each certification is a stake in the ground for financial transition.

### Plan 3: Somatic Experience Curator (The Wild Plan)
**Headline:** Cross-border performance, curation, and aesthetic presence  
**5-Year Arc:** Post-financial-independence → full-time movement performance, museum/gallery curating, international travel for shows and workshops  
**Confidence:** Low near-term, high long-term aspiration  
**What makes it work:** Integrates every dimension — aesthetic intelligence, body, curation, travel, teaching  
**What's honest about it:** Currently this is the destination, not the path. Plan 2 is the path.
