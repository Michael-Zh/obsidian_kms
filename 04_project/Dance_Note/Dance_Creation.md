## Improv Philosophy

- Improv is restricted creativity working within structure — a mix of predictable and unpredictable (like an approaching storm). Involves reading patterns, trials, feelings; if things don't work, find an alternative through movement or its opposite
- **Structure and constraint are not the enemy of freedom** — they're the container that makes freedom possible. Order, structure, rules provide a "secured ground" to explore from, not a cage to escape. This connects to the aesthetic preference in [[Aesthetic-Intelligence]]: drawn to work where formal discipline and sensory/symbolic density amplify each other
- **Creative boredom pattern**: Once I understand and know something well, I get bored. Want continuous learning/improvement. Bored in rehearsals and repeating performances; only interested when still learning
- **Work direction**: Create work based on improv to keep it fresh. Attend more workshops and performances related to improv-based creation

## Artist Identity

"Am I a professional dancer? How good is good? Am I good enough? Why do I want to do this? Do I need to justify this lifestyle?"

**Context**: Belgium perceived as having a more vibrant art scene than NL — more activities, more diversity/inclusion, more community engagement. Triggers comparison and identity questioning.

**Current framing**: Still working to establish identity as someone who has an office job AND is a dedicated artist. The answer may not be a single identity but a portfolio identity — both are true simultaneously.

**Disability dance reflection** (re: Anne Loes project): After initial connection with disabled bodies (moved by their stories, dedication, passion), felt drained. The project didn't leverage disabled bodies as artistic advantages — felt like a "dumbed-down version of normal." At this stage of artistic exploration, projects need to challenge and grow, not just move or inspire. Valid and honest criterion for project selection going forward.

## Improvisation Sustaining (from Uri, 2026-06-25)

Four principles for staying in flow during extended improvisation:

1. **Don't fear repetition** — intentional repetition is valid. If choosing to repeat, the choice itself makes it meaningful.
2. **Be clear about intention** — know what you're doing and why, even if the audience doesn't.
3. **Train for specific durations** — if you want to sustain flow for 1 or 5 minutes, train specifically for that length. Extended improv is a skill, not a state.
4. **Calibrate inner feeling vs. outer view** — internal sensation and what the audience sees are different. Develop your choreographic eye alongside your bodily feeling. Film yourself to close the gap.

**On creation:** Start small, create often. Extract material from what feels interesting → reflect → film → recombine. (ref: [[2026-06-25]])

## Artistic Inspiration — "Enemy of the People" Tribunal (June 2026)

*Holborn Festival, immersive tribunal format — June 24, 2026*

**Format strength**: Courtroom without authority figures (no judge, no clerk — only a coordinator). Audience is the jury. Some audience members selected to stage as real questioners and final voters.

**What made it work**:
- Rules existed but were deliberately bent — productive chaos that mirrored the subject matter
- Multiple layers of "the people": family, community, village, tourists, humanity — who counts?
- Symbol ≠ Reality: recorded clips of a person are not the person — structural tension of cancel culture
- Truth is multiple versions, not one fact — the prosecutor's closing monologue was his opening repeated verbatim

**Themes relevant to creative practice**:
- Authority without authority: No one had formal power, but deference patterns emerged anyway
- The whistleblower structure: speaking truth costs the individual everything; the system may still not hear
- Verdict multi-dimensionality: guilty at community scale, innocent at humanity scale — both true simultaneously
