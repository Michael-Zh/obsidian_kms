# Ballet Notes

Personal corrections and cues from class, organized by movement. Cross-references to CT Toolbox are noted as `[[CT_Toolbox#^block-id]]` where relevant.

## Warm-up Sequence
*(Class structure — procedural)*

1. Warm-up walk, point, slow tendu and plié, demi rond, head roll and body bend, parallel stretch
2. Free plié
3. Tendu to plié, drag back
4. Quick tendu en croix, balance on coupé back
5. Two small développés and one enveloppé en croix, front leg coupé balance
6. Jeté, two ballonnés en croix, retiré balance back
7. Pike en croix four-four-two-two-three en cloche
8. Passé to back to front, plié rond, four ronds de jambe, battement enveloppé, grand rond, jeté. Free stretch to arabesque balance
9. Frappé front-side-front, quarter rond to side then front, double en croix, petit battement, 2x8 attitude balance
10. Fondu enveloppé stretch, fondu retiré, back, side, rond en l'air back to retiré
11. Développé plié, rise, front-back-side, promenade to other side
12. Grand battement, two ballonnés, front-back-side, en croix en cloche
13. Calf rises

## Floor Barre

- Lengthen the lower back in the L-shaped seated position
- In first position: touch the calf to check extension; use back/outside of the leg (okay to lift leg off floor as long as the body is long and ribs are closed)
- When flexing: toes are flat
- Sixth position should touch both the toe and the ankle

---

## Barre

- Watch for barre shoulder pulling too far forward
- Keep the mid back neutral; avoid the pelvis tilting forward. Check if neck and head can move freely
- Center side of side body pulls down and out — feel the support under the armpit and slightly back (the traps)
- Don't let the arm close too much in port de bras — it pulls the shoulder forward
- Be aware of the space around you; feel the ripple from movement initiation (especially upper body)
- When lifting legs higher than 45 degrees behind: to keep the upper body upright, move the barre-side hand slightly forward
- Feel the weight in a big fourth to prepare for turns
- Balance on the plié to feel the position and check if anything is blocked
- From second parallel: forward, turn out and plié — feel the underside of the thigh and roll up (back side of the body)
- Tendu: feel the lift and length of the standing leg — directly transfers to turns
- In plié rond de jambe: keep the upper body and hip position, only épaulement changes
- Long toes in attitude and arabesque
- Lift the lats and traps on the standing side to keep balance
- In forward bend: lift the sit bone up and forward, use the turn-out muscle, and pull the shoulder blades toward the hips

---

## Port de Bras

- Elbow down and heavy; drop the shoulder blades to send the arm downward — creates a soft, continuous line → see [[CT_Toolbox#^ct-d-reduce-tension]] ^ballet-pdb-elbow-down
- Arms like brushing on the surface of water when in front
- 1st to 3rd (lifting up): back of the hand leads
- 3rd to 5th (opening from top): back of the hand wipes the window
- 5th to 3rd (closing from top): palm wipes the window
- Put the shoulder in line with your back, not your torso
- Think: elongate the left pinky finger, and right middle finger *(personal feel)*
- Touch the sternum to feel a soft yet lifted chest — makes a difference for posture
- Carry the whole body through each port de bras — don't leave parts behind

---

## Turn-out

- Find the lengthening of the top of the glute — the pull is from underneath. Pelvis neutral, especially in deep pliés
- Release the groin and feel the leg bone deep in the hip socket; as you bend the knee, the ball rotates in the socket

---

## Tendu

- Feel the IT band lengthen, connecting to the toes and the side body
- Barre-side pelvis bone forward and center-side shoulder forward; center-side pelvis bone back and barre-side shoulder back *(épaulement alignment)*

---

## Turns

*Personal corrections:*
- Right arm tends to lock
- Left shoulder tends to move forward and lift
- Ribs tend to open and lift, causing the back to over-arch

*Technique cues:*
- Feel everything stacked on each other, balanced on the 2nd/3rd toe
- Feel there's an air pocket or egg in the front of the shoulder joint, in front of the armpit — don't break it (avoid over-crossing)
- When turning, keep the shape of the arms. Don't throw the arms too far back
- Actively close the arm after allongé together with the body; connect through the inner arm to inner thigh through heels
- Shoulders and body still; arms just float upward. Keep the arc of the arm slightly smaller, tracing in front of the body
- Fix the shoulders and back — don't let them move
- Back straight and on the hips, don't arch; hands up from allongé directly into fifth (keep chest down, arms not too far back); think from plié to relevé — not a kick or jump into it
- Find the sync of relevé and retiré — relevé shouldn't be so fast it causes a "jump" or jerk into position
- Passé and plié to feel the axis
- Rolling of the feet
- Lengthen the side body (especially left)
- Keep the turn-out before going to relevé
- Plié well and get up quickly; from relevé, roll down to plié to end the turn
- Think about the knee chasing toward the direction of the turn
- More plié in préparation — release into it and think of a spring
- In balance: think of the top of the shoulder bone pulling down toward the lower back
- Release the toes; drop the shoulder blades and find the connection between upper and mid back
- En dedans turn préparation: make sure the upper back is supported and upright, not over-arching

## Balance

- Check if the hip and ribcage are stacked — sometimes the pelvis lifts sideways to compensate and the ribcage sinks

---

## Attitude

- Bend the knee a bit more
- Feel that your upper front is leaning on a yoga ball in front of you
- Lift for attitude back: think the knee traces a circle
- Lift from the back of the knee
- Square the hip
- Use the shoulder blades to support the upper back

---

## Arabesque

- Shoulders and body aligned, facing the diagonal
- Use the mid back rib to lift the upper body forward
- Arabesque arm line — collarbone is part of the line; shoulder blades pull down
- Lift the ribs to lengthen; let it run up and forward to make a C-shape (lower back is an elongated hinge, not a 90-degree angle)
- Shoulders and hips in one square — to use the power
- From plié, lift the leg to arabesque — think of a dart shooting outward, not up and over
- Put the shoulder in line with the back (more back than you think), but don't let the ribs fly out

---

## Développé

- Step développé: feel the lift rather than jerking into the pose
- In développé and fondu front: feel the lower back not tuck into the tailbone — avoids leaning back or crunching the back

---

## Grand Battement

- Temps lié — use the whole body, ripple up

---

## Grand Pirouette

- Connect the allongé arms (inner arm) to side ribs and inner thigh; shoulder not overly externally rotated but kept back
- First arm closes in first; stay in plié until en face, then open and go on demi-pointe

---

## Ballonné

- Glue/press the working leg tight to the standing leg on the bend

---

## Soutenu

- Soutenu is a set of micro steps, not a drag

---

## Jeté

- Lead with elbow, not the hand — keeps the upper body shape. Also go through first to brush out

---

## Sissonne

- Watch the back shoulder down
- Knee to side, keep the diamond shape in plié

---

## Lame Duck

- Feel the arms go with the leg for the lift

---

## Tour en l'Air

- More plié
- Keep the arm in front — don't let it suddenly drop

---

## Beats

- Don't sit; open more (Russian style)

---

## Pike Relevé

- To front and up
- Feel from the back of the bottom of the rib cage: the leg originates from there; contraction is from there. Shoulder just relaxes and sits on the back — no need to pull very hard

---

## Sous-sus

- Lead with the feet but bring the whole body toward the balance

## Nutrition for Dancers (working full-time) (ref: [[HOW TO EAT LIKE AN ATHLETE  BALLET DIET|Ballet Diet]])

- **Regular carb-rich meals with protein** — don't skip or under-fuel. Underfueling causes impaired concentration and injury risk, not just fatigue.
- **Pre-class fueling**: Eat a proper meal 1.5–2h before class. For a 7pm class: eat at 5–5:30pm at work. Light snack OK 30–45 min before if needed.
- **Post-class glycogen window**: 45-minute window after class for recovery meal. Carbs + protein. Don't wait too long post-class — this directly feeds the sleep/late-eating problem.
- **The adaptation for full-time job scenario**: The challenge isn't knowledge — it's timing. Pre-class eating at work (5–5:30pm) solves both: (1) prevents post-class hunger crash, (2) enables earlier eating schedule so sleep isn't delayed.
- **Rest day fueling**: Still eat properly on rest days — recovery happens on rest days, not training days. Don't restrict on non-training days.
- **Body image note**: The professional dancer standard is not the relevant comparison for a recreational/training context. Fueling for performance, not aesthetics.
