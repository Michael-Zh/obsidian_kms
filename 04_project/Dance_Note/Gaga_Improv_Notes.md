# Gaga / Improv Notes

Notes from Gaga classes and contemporary improv sessions. Gaga is a movement language developed by Ohad Naharin — it works primarily through imagery and sensation rather than technical correction.

---

## Warm-up Sequence

1. Ankle/feet circles, point and flex, alternating
2. Feet massage
3. Hip hug, hook and circle, shake
4. Knee in, shake and drop
5. Pound knee with flex feet
6. Hand shake on the side; work on dropping the shoulder (tense then relax), go faster
7. Lay down in savasana — make everything quiet
8. Hands on belly, then drop to the floor as a gesture of letting go
9. Small movement — be sensitive, like entering a new body; be curious
10. Laying on the side
11. Child's pose
12. Head heavy to tabletop, tilt the spine, bigger movement, shifting weight on one leg/hand
13. Find stretch
14. Down dog, bend over, belly on the thigh
15. Use shoulder blades to move the arm
16. Leading with one body part, delay of movements
17. Keep shifting weight and bend the hip to catch the weight

## Principles

- Let yourself break the skin
- Use the eyes — when balancing, not toward the floor
- Reverse the movement to break habit
- Tiredness is like rain you can't change — but you can enjoy it
- Open your heart to let emotion colour your movement
- Open your skin — be vulnerable
- Connect to pleasure
- Try to find movement and flow even when in relevé
- Use momentum to prepare for the next move — find the letting go
- Use body parts to feel the floor, skin, air. When touched, let the touched part touch back — activate it
- Take time to receive a message and react — don't always need to react immediately
- Follow instinct rather than overthinking
- Face muscles can be choreographed and worked out as a means of expression, just like any other body part

## Body & Sensing

- Spine has many pieces — it's not one rigid column
- Use another engine to power the movement — e.g. arm over head with the lats
- Use the pelvis to move the leg; feel its weight in all fours
- As the hands come from the gut to manipulate the head, and the other way around
- The bones meeting the flesh — the excitement
- Collapse the core — let it be soft, not braced
- Get used to the feeling of falling; stretching out the body helps the fall reach the ground
- Completely melt to the floor
- Grab the bones; use the muscles around the bone to generate speed
- Grab the bones in one part, but relax the rest
- Separate the sit bone and heel/feet: close gap vs. separate; or close both to soften the knee
- Seat bone close and open to each other
- Find small places to ignite (activate)
- Feel the difference between activating vs. not
- Roll around after stretching the legs; activate hips
- Toe walk to wake up the toes
- Touch collarbone, tip of shoulder, shoulder blades — explore the range; let that ripple to the hands
- As you massage the feet, bring movement to other parts of the body to feel the same sensation
- Happy Baby pose: keep the connection and move the hips around
- Find continuous movement — not blocking, even while still
- Pivot the toes before falling
- Identify the time difference; catch up along the movement, not just at checkpoints — follow the same weight distribution
- Light out and use the floor

## Movement Qualities & Imagery

- Tight and loose skin — feel the contrast to explore different textures of movement
- Grapes not raisins — a quality metaphor: full and plump vs. shrunken and dried
- Feel the drop with gravity of a body part, then find the elasticity — drop-and-catch, like catching yourself from falling asleep
- Make sound from the mouth — like raindrops when many people do it together
- As if floating, keep it light
- Let pressured water or air float through you
- Spread the nice smell around you — spatial metaphor for expanding reach and presence
- Actively drinking water vs. letting water flow through the throat — different imagery changes how you move
- Think of the chest as a box; arms grow from there until they disappear
- Multiple balls of different sizes, qualities, and materials — imagine your body is made of these; switch the parameters to get different textures of movement

## Gaga Exercises

- **Belly wave**: touch two points on the belly; imagine sucking in from one point up, back, and around to the other — making a wave. Activates the belly and opens movement possibilities
- **Yoyo ball**: think of the pelvis as a yoyo — pelvic floor lifts but also drops down; belly sucks in and up — the two directions pull away from each other
- **Armpits**: cancel the space between armpits, legs, toes — but keep moving. Or add imagined objects between armpits (ping pong → tennis ball → orange → melon) — varying size changes body shape
- **Circle and place**: from once, to gesture, to multiple locations
- **Contact and response**: touch one part of the body and move a specific other part in response
- **Spine twist**: side and front; twist the spine from base to top, or vice versa
- **External and internal force**: let the force take you to forms
- **Vibration**: find vibrating patterns to break — vary direction, frequency, volume, initiation point
- **1–10 intensity**: small gesture to full expression; always start multiple, don't need to finish
