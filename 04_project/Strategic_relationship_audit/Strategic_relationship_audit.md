## Three-Category Needs Taxonomy

*Written June 2026 — input for ENM SLA design and partner conversations.*

The needs this relationship context creates are not monolithic. They fall into three distinct categories — each with different emotional drivers, different conversation requirements, and different solution architectures.

### Category 1: Somatic / Closeness Need
**What it is:** Skin-to-skin contact as primary — physical warmth, holding, body closeness. This is not act-specific or arousal-driven. It is connection through physical presence.

**Where it comes from:** Identified June 2026 as a *third* category distinct from the other two. Previously unnamed, which meant it was being misattributed (as part of novelty or BDSM drive).

**What it requires:** A partner who is physically warm and present. The partner's sensory sensitivity (low-touch preference) creates a direct mismatch here. This need is partially met and cannot be easily outsourced — it is intrinsically relational, not transactional.

**Conversation it needs:** Honest acknowledgment that this gap exists. Question for self: Is the somatic closeness need something a long-term primary partner must fulfill, or can it be met in part through other close relationships (non-romantic physical warmth, bodywork, dance)?

---

### Category 2: Novelty / ENM
**What it is:** Meeting interesting people, the energy of new attraction, the experience of being seen and desired by someone new. Fun, lightness, exploration. The "being free to explore without losing security" need.

**Where it comes from:** Long-standing pattern; named clearly in multiple coaching sessions. Distinct from physical drive — this is about relational novelty and the aliveness of new connection.

**What it requires:** An ENM framework with explicit scope and communication protocol. Not high frequency — quality of connection matters more than quantity.

**Conversation it needs:** The SLA negotiation — scope, frequency caps, communication level (full data vs. need-to-know). This is the most socially complex category because it requires partner consent.

---

### Category 3: BDSM / Control-Experimentation
**What it is:** Exploration of power dynamics, dominance/submission, deliberate intensity. This is not the same as the novelty drive — it is specific to a type of experience that requires a particular partner capacity.

**Where it comes from:** The "Power/Deficit Gap" — partner has medical constraints; this need is currently outsourced (Grindr/saunas), creating "system noise" (guilt).

**What it requires:** Either a partner who can engage with this dimension (current partner cannot), or an ENM structure that explicitly accommodates this as a separate outsourced category, destigmatized.

**Conversation it needs:** Least urgent of the three for the cohabitation conversation — it is already partially externalized. But it needs to be named in the ENM SLA as a distinct category so it doesn't get bundled with and contaminated by Category 2 conversations.

---

### Synthesis: What This Taxonomy Does

Naming these as three distinct categories does structural work:
1. It prevents conflating novelty guilt ("I'm just restless") with somatic deficit ("I need to be held") with specific kink needs ("I need a different experience type") — these are different problems requiring different responses
2. It lets you enter the partner conversation with precision: you're not saying "I need more" (vague, threatening); you're saying "here is specifically what I need, in which category, and what I'm proposing"
3. It clarifies which needs the partner *can* address vs. which need the ENM structure to accommodate

**The cohabitation test:** Before October 2026 (Jeroen apartment), answer: Which of these three categories is a hard requirement from a primary partner? Which can be accommodated via ENM? Which could be met in other ways?
