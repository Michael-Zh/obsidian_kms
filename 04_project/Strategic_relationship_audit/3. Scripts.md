# Tactical Implementation: Scripts & Sensory Data Collection

## I. The "I Statement" Script: The Saturday Soft-Launch

**Objective:** To introduce the need for transparency and the ENM proposal without triggering a "Crisis Mode" response.

### 1. The Opening (Establishing Safety)

_Wait for a calm moment (e.g., over coffee). Do not lead with "We need to talk."_

> "I’ve been thinking a lot about our move-in and how much I value what we've built over these three years. I want our new home to be a place where we both feel completely safe and understood, especially with the stress of the house and the world right now."

### 2. The Vulnerability (The "Bug" in the Code)

_Transition to your internal state to avoid sounding like an accusation._

> "Lately, I’ve realized I’m carrying some guilt. Because my physical needs—like my sex drive and my need for touch—are higher than what we’ve been able to navigate together, I’ve found myself looking at apps or fantasizing just to cope. Doing that secretly makes me feel disconnected from you, and I hate that feeling. It feels like a 'bug' in my system that I want to fix before we share a roof."

### 3. The "Option C" Proposal (Transparency > Decision)

_Focus on the process of honesty rather than the finality of an 'Open Relationship' label._

> "I’m not asking for a final decision on an open relationship today, because I know you're already stressed. What I am asking for is a commitment to **Radical Transparency**. I want to be able to tell you when I’m feeling frustrated or when I’ve looked at an app, without it being a 'fight.' I want us to build a protocol where I can be honest about my 'Collector' nature, so it doesn't turn into a secret that erodes our trust."

## II. The Sensory Audit: Hardware Capacity for Kids

**Objective:** To gather objective data on whether his "system" can handle the 24/7 sensory input of a child.

As an INTP, use this for **Passive Observation** over the next 30 days. Do not tell him you are auditing him; simply record the data.

### 1. Tactile Threshold (Touch)

- **The Trigger:** A prolonged "High-Dosage" touch session (e.g., 15 mins of cuddling).
    
- **The Data Point:** Does he pull away? Does his breathing change? How long does he need "alone time" afterward to reset?
    
- **Kid Translation:** A child will want to climb, pull, and touch for 12+ hours a day. If his reset time for 15 mins of touch is >1 hour, his system will likely crash under 24/7 parenting.
    

### 2. Auditory/Chaos Threshold (Noise & Pivot)

- **The Trigger:** Unexpected noise or a sudden change in plans (e.g., a last-minute cancellation or loud construction nearby).
    
- **The Data Point:** Does he "shut down" (become quiet/withdrawn) or "melt down" (become irritable)?
    
- **Kid Translation:** Children are "Random Noise Generators." If he needs a structured routine to avoid stress, the "Spontaneity" of a child will keep him in a state of permanent Cortisol (Stress) spikes.
    

### 3. Recovery Time ($T_r$)

- **The Logic:** All parents get overstimulated. The question is: **How fast do you bounce back?**
    
- **Observation:** After a heavy day of house-related decisions, does he have any energy left for connection?
    
- **Verdict:** If he is "empty" after a standard day of administrative stress, he has no "overhead" for the additional labor of parenting.
