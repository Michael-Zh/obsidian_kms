# Session 2026-05-21 — Protocol Design Discussion

**Session type:** Discussion — no workout data

## Key Issues Identified
- Eating window (12:00–20:00) misaligned with evening training schedule → no post-workout nutrition, 14+ hour fast into next morning, low morning energy affecting full-time job
- Late-night reactive eating (sugary food) and insufficient protein identified as likely causes of fat% stall — downstream symptom of the window mismatch, not a separate discipline problem
- Protein adherence unverified — 3-day food log still pending

## Decisions Made
1. **Activity-aligned 16:8 protocol adopted** — window follows training schedule, not the clock:
   - Evening training days: 13:00–21:00 (recovery meal right after training; sleep 23:30–00:00)
   - Morning training days (Mysore, ballet): 08:00–16:00 or 09:00–17:00 (sleep 23:00)
   - Rest / gym days: 12:00–20:00 or 13:00–21:00
2. **Post-workout meal is lighter on evening training days** — 400–500 kcal protein + fast carbs, not the full 900 kcal dinner; bigger meal at window open (13:00)
3. **No regular morning + evening doubles** — energy crash pattern documented; occasional fine, systematic doubles would undermine recovery
4. **Staying on 16:8, not switching to 12:12** — body composition target (15%→9% BFM) requires the longer fasted period for meaningful fat oxidation benefit

## Food for Thought
- The 3–4 hour pre-sleep rule is about volume/heaviness, not just timing — a lighter 400–500 kcal recovery meal at 21:00 before midnight sleep is workable
- Reactive late-night eating was a symptom of the window mismatch — fixing the window should eliminate it without needing separate nutrition discipline
- Once window is stable (~1 week), do the 3-day food log to verify protein adherence before declaring nutrition variable open
