# Session 2026-06-02 — Schedule Restructure: Sun Ballet + Mon/Tue Gym

**Session type:** Program-level schedule restructure

## Context
Feedback from execution: "workout after ballet class almost never work." The old Monday double (ADC Ballet 19:00 → Gym ~21:00) consistently failed due to energy crash — ballet 🔴 + office day + gym 🔴 was unsustainable. This triggered a full day-structure review.

## What Was Changed

### Day Structure (Normal Week)

| Day | Old | New |
|-----|-----|-----|
| **Sun** | Gym (back/legs/arms) | ADC Ballet 16:00 |
| **Mon** | ADC Ballet 19:00 → Gym after (double🔴) | Gym (back/legs/arms) + Iyengar 20:15 (🔴+🟢) |
| **Tue** | Salsa+Hiphop bundle or Contemporary | Gym (chest/shoulder) + optional light class (🔴+🟡) |
| **Wed** | ADC Jazz ± Hiphop | ADC Jazz ± Hiphop (unchanged) |
| **Thu** | Iyengar 18:30 (� fixed) | ADC Ballet 17:30 or HJS (flex, fallback to Iyengar) |
| **Fri** | Mysore or Rest | Mysore or Rest (unchanged) |
| **Sat** | Rest | Rest (unchanged) |

### D loaded Week — New Two-variant Design

**Variant A (non-chreo weeks: Wed Iyengar):** Sun ballet → Mon half gym → T eformer → Wed Iyengar → Thu Yin

**Variant B (chreo weeks 2+4: Wed Jazz):** Sun ballet → Mon Iyengar → Tue half gym → Wed Jazz → Thu Yin

Both variants include 1x ballet + 1x half gym + 1x Iyengar + 1x Reformer + 1x Yin. No Mysore, no HJS, no doubles.

### 3x Gym Question — Settled
Decision: No. 2x gym is sufficient for muscle retention during a caloric deficit. A third session would add recovery cost without proportional fat-loss benefit (that's driven by dietary compliance, not volume). If wanting more metabolic stimulus: add 8-10 min finisher after each gym session instead.

## Food for Thought
- The new structure still has 4 potential High days (Sun+Mon+Tue+Thu) — if Friday Mysore is added, either Thu ballet or Fri Mysore must drop. This effectively enforces a choice between a 4-day or 5-day High week, which is a healthy constraint.
- Corss-day energy data on the new structure will be the real test — specifically whether Monday gym (no prior double) delivers better performance than the old Monday ballet+gym stack
- D loaded week now has two clear branches gated by the choreo cycle — this is tighter than the old single template and removes ambiguity at planning time

# Session 2026-06-02 — Nutrition Restructure & Scan Validation

**Session type:** Protocol refinement + Scan analysis

## Context & Scan Data
June 2 Body Composition Scan validated the May schedule fix (removing the Monday double + aligning the fasting window).
* **Data:** 89.1kg | 43.6kg SMM (+1.1kg) | 13.1kg BFM (-1.3kg) | 14.8% Body Fat. Phase angle improved to 7.1. Flawless recomposition.
* **Friction Identified:** Eating a massive 900-calorie solid dinner *after* evening training (21:30) places a massive digestive load too close to the 23:00–00:00 sleep window. The Thermic Effect of Food (TEF) from heavy solid protein keeps core temperature high, destroying Deep Sleep architecture and CNS recovery.

## What Was Changed: The "Tri-Phasic" Window
Shifted from a 2-meal heavy structure to a 3-phase strategic structure to protect sleep and fuel dance performance, keeping the 2300 kcal target (175g P / 230g C / 75g F).

1. **Phase 1 (12:00/13:00) - The Anchor:** Heaviest solid protein (250g meat) + fats. Focus: stable energy for desk work.
2. **Phase 2 (17:30) - The Primer:** Carb-forward, moderate/light protein (pasta/rice, light fish/tofu). Focus: explosive fuel for evening training; must digest quickly.
3. **Phase 3 (21:30) - The Soft Close:** Liquid/soft protein heavy (Whey Isolate, Skyr, Creatine). Focus: fast absorption, prevents core temperature elevation before 23:00/00:00 sleep.

## Key Decisions
1. Sleep boundary strictly enforced: In bed by 23:00 or midnight maximum.
2. Nutrition tracking variable unlocked: Protein column in `Training_running_log.md` is now active and must be tracked to ensure the 175g target is hit across the 3 phases.
