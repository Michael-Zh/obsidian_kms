## ENM Framework: Rupture-and-Repair as Core Diagnostic (June 2026)

- (new) **Rupture-and-repair > structure (ref: [[Open Versus Closed Why Gay Men Are So Divided On Relationships]], 2026-06-07)**: Alexis Caught's thesis: how a couple navigates conflict and repairs it matters more than whether they're monogamous or open. The ENM SLA is a container for practicing rupture-and-repair, not a destination. The real pre-conversation diagnostic: can this partnership hold ambiguity and come back from it?
- (new) **Invitation framing vs. request framing (ref: [[Open Versus Closed Why Gay Men Are So Divided On Relationships]], 2026-06-07)**: Leading with "what do *you* actually want in this relationship?" (joint inquiry) is less triggering than "I want to open things up" (pre-formed request). Jeroen (ISFJ, autistic tendencies, Dutch context) has likely never interrogated his monogamy. Entry point: invite his inquiry, not present a demand.
- (new) **Emotional regulation as prerequisite (ref: [[Open Versus Closed Why Gay Men Are So Divided On Relationships]], 2026-06-07)**: Non-monogamy is more easily navigated by people who can hold ambiguity and regulate strong emotional responses. Concrete probe for the disclosure conversation: not just "are you ok with ENM?" but "can we practice sitting with discomfort together and coming back?"
- (new) **Physical vs. emotional monogamy distinction (ref: [[Open Versus Closed Why Gay Men Are So Divided On Relationships]], 2026-06-07)**: The FWB kiss triggered physical rejection (body naturally said no). May indicate higher emotional monogamy than the ENM framing implies. Internal question to clarify: is the need for outside encounters primarily *somatic/touch deficit* or *novelty/BDSM/curiosity*? Different answers = different SLA designs.
## Open vs. Closed — Gay Relationship Framing (ref: [[Open Versus Closed Why Gay Men Are So Divided On Relationships|Open vs Closed]])

- (new) **Why the debate is so charged**: Gay men have historically had their relationships invalidated (not "real" love) by mainstream society. This means both open and monogamous relationship choices carry shame narratives — judgement about moral laxity (open) OR capitulating to hetero-normative standards (closed). Neither side is free from external projection.
- (new) **Evolutionary framing**: Natural variation exists in humans, not a single "correct" default. Evolutionary biology does not prescribe monogamy as the universal design.
- (new) **Reasons couples open relationships**: Desire discrepancy (mismatch in libido/appetite), individual expression needs, reclaiming shame around sexuality. This connects directly to the touch appetite asymmetry with Jeroen.
- (new) **Rupture-and-repair over structure**: The therapeutic principle is that relationship quality depends more on how couples handle conflict and reconnection (rupture-and-repair) than on whether they are open or closed. Structure is less predictive of success than communication and repair capacity.
- (new) **What this means for current situation**: The open vs. closed question is ultimately less important than: can the two of you repair well when things go wrong? The debate about ENM / touch needs is a proxy — what matters is whether there's a reliable repair cycle.
