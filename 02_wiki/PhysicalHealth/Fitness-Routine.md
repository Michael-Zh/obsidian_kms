## Gradual Change Philosophy

**Key Principle**: Change only ONE thing per month — minimal, gradual, sustainable (ref: [[2026-05-11]], [[2026-05-13]])

**Priority order for monthly change:**
1. Sleeping time + fasting and meal timing (foundation, affects everything else)
2. What to eat (quality and composition)
3. Training routine changes (e.g., contemporary, salsa solo, alternating Pilates and Iyengar)

**Why this works**: Changing too many things at once makes it impossible to identify what's working. One variable per month creates a real feedback loop.

## Technical Reference — Deadlift Five Steps

- (new) **Trap bar vs conventional**: Primarily working on trap bar deadlift (significantly lower lower back impact). Conventional deadlift principles still useful as technical foundation (ref: [[2026-05-29_硬拉]])
- (new) **The five steps** (from 《力量训练基础》Ch.4): (1) Stance — hip-width, not squat stance; (2) Grip — hands just outside legs, not wide; (3) Shin contact — knee moves forward toward bar, not hips drop; (4) Big chest / lock back — maintain rigid spine; (5) Drag up along legs — don't move the bar
- (new) **Core principle**: Don't look for feel first — position first. Feeling comes after position is correct. Compensation masquerades as "feel" (ref: [[2026-05-29_硬拉]])

## Over-Planning Pattern (Meta-Level)

- (new) **"Perfect plan fallacy" (2026-06-02)**: Tendency to over-plan — as if planning it perfectly will make execution perfect. But in reality, execution and prioritization are the real challenges. Too many accidents and changes mean the plan is a template, not a script. Planning is useful but should not substitute for adaptive execution.
- **Connection**: This same pattern appears in work (over-analyzing the methodology before presenting) and relationships (designing the perfect ENM protocol before having the conversation). The engine is identical: anxiety managed through more planning.

## Deload Protocol Refinement (June 2026)

- (new) **Deload standard (from [[运动科学中的"减负周"（Deload Week）标准指南]])**: Use 80% of normal load while maintaining original rep/set count — do NOT reduce sets/reps. Better stimulus for recovery than reducing volume. Requires trade-off assessment. (ref: [[2026-06-22]])

## Sleep & Eating — Two-Track Protocol (from coaching_20260620)

- (new) **Root cause diagnosis**: Sleep failure is structural, not motivational. Two structural conflicts: (1) phone is the only wind-down tool with no end condition; (2) training window 6–8:30pm creates pre-class hunger gap → post-class overeating → late/heavy eating → delayed sleep
- (new) **Two-track protocol**:
  - *Track A* — home by 10:30pm: shower + heat food → eat → 30-min phone timer → bed (target: 11pm–12am)
  - *Track B* — home after 10:30pm (Mon Iyengar, Thu sauna): normal wind-down, no sleep target — remove from failure account
- (new) **Pre-class eating**: On training days, eat a proper meal at work before leaving (5–5:30pm): rice/sandwich + protein. Prevents post-class hunger crash and late-night overeating.
- (new) **Sunday Meal Prep Protocol (20-min passive)**: After gym session — (1) start rice cooker (4–5 days of rice), (2) boil 8–10 eggs, (3) batch one protein source (chicken/tofu/tin fish, oven or pan). All run while showering.
