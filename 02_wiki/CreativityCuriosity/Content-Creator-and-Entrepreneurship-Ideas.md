## Movement Lab Entrepreneur Concept

**Origin**: Synthesizing different movement disciplines (ballet, contemporary, jazz, yoga, pilates) into customized practices

**Problem Solved**: "One specific practice may be too rigid; not everything works for my body and needs. I need to understand what I need (awareness + goal) then look for elements from different things to experiment with." (ref: [[2026-01-30]])

**Categorization Framework**:
1. **Goal-Based**: Performance (speed, explosive, control, endurance, strength, agility), pain relief, mobility/flexibility
2. **Practice-Specific**: Teaching genre principles (ballet, yoga, dance, etc.)
3. **Principle-Teaching**: CT (Corrective Training?) and other frameworks

**Format Options**: Online coaching, studio workshops, retreat-style intensives

## Business Philosophy

**Core Principle** (from 2016-12-16): "Business is a tool, a way to achieve the goal. If your goal is something higher than you, business can be very beneficial." (ref: [[2016-12-16]])

**Implementation Strategy**: Keep full-time job and explore. Don't rush into either/or decisions. "If I feel ready to do this full-time or I'm ready, I will know the time and go." (ref: [[2016-12-16]])

## Success Metrics

Not "biggest" or "most profitable," but:
- Does it serve real need?
- Does it feel authentic (from true heart)?
- Am I learning and growing?
- Can I maintain it without overwhelming myself?

"Don't need to be the best, but relatively good at something, and offer unique combination of skills" (ref: [[2016-12-16]])

## Solo Developer / Indie Maker Reference

**Mark Lou model** (ref: 文章/2026-05-21 — 月入7.7万美元的独立开发者):
- $77k/month solo, 35 startups total, 30 didn't work — ~5% hit rate
- Core insight: **It's not about being smarter or better at picking ideas. It's about rolling the dice more times than everyone else.** Ship fast, fail fast, keep going.
- Works 365 days a year by choice — not because he has to, but because he'd repeat the same day if he could
- Most people fail not from lack of ideas but from treating each idea as too precious to ship
- The real competitive advantage: volume of attempts, not quality of selection

**Why this resonates**: Aligns with the 90% abandonment rate in POS. Also directly challenges the pattern of over-refining ideas before shipping — a known friction point. The "dice rolling" metaphor is actionable: lower the cost of each attempt, increase frequency.

**Applied to portfolio career**: Don't need each creative/entrepreneurial bet to be perfect. Need more small bets, faster feedback loops.
