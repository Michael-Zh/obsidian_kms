# Fighting Monkey (Jozef Frucek & Linda Kapetanea)

## Who They Are

Contemporary dance and martial arts practitioners turned movement system architects. Based in Europe. Created Fighting Monkey (FM) — not a technique but a practice environment designed around uncertainty, ageing, and adaptability. The central question: how does a body stay functional and alive in conditions it hasn't been trained for?

## Core Philosophy

- **Teach situations, not moves.** FM designs "Movement Situations" (games, constraints, partner tasks) that force the body to self-organize. The learning happens in the problem-solving, not the repetition.
- **Chaos as the training medium.** Predictable drills produce brittle skills. Exposure to unpredictability builds genuine adaptability — antifragility applied to the body.
- **Structure serves communication and survival.** The underlying rule is cognitive minimalism: you don't need to memorize 200 muscle activations if you understand the one principle that governs them all.
- **Ageing as part of the practice.** FM explicitly addresses what happens to movement capacity over decades — unusual in a field that typically ignores this.

## Business Model

- **Global high-margin workshops:** Weekend intensives, invite-only or limited capacity. Ticket scarcity maintained deliberately.
- **Online knowledge products:** "Earthquake Architecture" and other series — asynchronous, packaged, passive income.
- **Proprietary tools:** FM-specific training equipment (wooden balls, resistance bands) — extends the brand and deepens the moat.

## Resonance with Empowerment Architect Profile

Fighting Monkey is the closest existing model to the "find the pattern, don't memorize the output" cognitive mode. Their pedagogical logic (situations → self-organization → principles) is structurally identical to reverse-engineering a manager's decisions into a decision knowledge base. The translation direction is different; the underlying method is the same.
