# [[Movement-Career-Options]]

## Portfolio Career: Teaching & Movement Practice Feedback Loop

**Core Joy**: Teaching motivates you to go deeper in practice; whatever you learn nurtures teaching. This feedback loop is energizing and sustainable. (ref: [[coaching_discussion_20251104]])

Why this matters: Movement careers don't have to be all-or-nothing transitions away from corporate life. They can be integrated as deep, meaningful part of portfolio.

## Certification Options vs. Master's Degree

### Recommended Path: Professional Certifications

**Iyengar Certification**
- Deep, personal dive into Iyengar method
- Feeds teaching practice at yoga studios
- Can be integrated with current corporate role
- Teaching hours can scale (local, online, etc.)
- Advantage: not all-or-nothing like Master's

**Gyrokinesis Certification**
- Advanced movement system certification
- Often offered in modules (not full-time commitment)
- Enables teaching of specialized movement
- Can build client base locally or online
- Advantage: modular, integrates with existing schedule

**Benefit of Certifications**: They satisfy the desire for "professional level" and "learning depth" without requiring life restructure. Teaching becomes the practice, practice feeds teaching.

### Alternative: Dance Therapy Master's

**Critical Questions to Ask First**:
1. Are you being **pulled** toward dance therapy by genuine desire to be a therapist?
   - Have you researched what day-to-day life of dance therapist actually looks like?
   - Is _that_ the calling you feel?
2. Or are you being **pushed** by need to make dance "productive" to justify it?
   - This is a red flag; suggests solving the wrong problem
   - Therapy work is emotionally/mentally demanding in its own right

**If You Choose Master's Path**:
- Ensure it's genuine calling, not escape route
- Understand it's demanding (similar intensity to corporate career)
- Consider: does it align with location-independence goal?

## The "Portfolio" in Practice

Not "replace job" → Instead: "switch between things so I don't get bored"

Examples:
- Some weeks: focus on corporate work
- Some weeks: deepen movement practice or teaching
- Some weeks: create (choreograph, develop classes, compose)
- Monthly cycles: prioritize one thing, then another

Flexibility maintains joy without forcing choice.

## Physical Timeline Awareness

"What if I don't practice dance deliberately, my age will be stopping me from going further" — valid reality for intensive movement. (ref: [[coaching_discussion_20251104]])

This creates genuine urgency (not FOMO). But urgency suggests:
- Don't delay professional certifications
- But also: corporate stability finances deep practice (current strength)
- Question: what's realistic 20-year vision? Teaching? Creation? Performance?

## Integration with Corporate

Corporate career provides:
- Financial freedom to train multiple styles without worrying about income
- Location flexibility (if made independent)
- Intellectual stimulation that feeds creativity
- Stability that reduces stress around movement work

Movement provides:
- Physical/somatic intelligence (complements analytical mind)
- Creative outlet
- Teaching/mentoring (different from corporate delivery)
- Community and human connection

## Reading & Learning Approach

**New Framework for Information Intake** (ref: [[Information-Overload]]):
- Read a few pages even if not feeling it; goal is finding inspirational parts for later processing
- Don't force reading every page or from page one
- Read what feels alive; skip when bored
- Don't worry about memorizing; make small notes on inspirational sections

This honors your natural learning style (curiosity-driven, not forced) while still building knowledge.

## Life Design Framework: The 3-Plan Architecture (ref: [[Life_Design_Coaching_Transcript]])

**(new) From DYL coaching session (June 2026)**:

- **Plan 1** — Continue corporate track; develop intuition by reverse-engineering manager's judgment into structured knowledge base (not copying her style)

**Key reframe**: Curation IS high-level creativity — selecting, synthesizing, and growing seeds to trees is rare and valuable. The inner critic saying "I'm not creative enough" is misidentifying capability type.
