# [[Identity-Based-Life-Philosophy]]

## Core Insight: Identity as Foundation (ref: [[2026-02-22]])

**The Problem**: New Year's resolutions fail because people try to change behavior without changing identity.

**The Mechanism**:
1. You set a goal (behavioral level)
2. You use goal as lens to perceive reality
3. You notice only "important" info for achieving goal (learning)
4. You act toward goal, get feedback
5. You repeat until behavior becomes automatic (conditioning)
6. Behavior becomes part of your identity ("I'm someone who...") (ref: [[2026-02-22]])

**The Truth**: If you want a result, you must first _become_ the kind of person who would have that result. Not through willpower, but through identity shift.

**Example**: A fit person doesn't "force" healthy eating through discipline. They naturally resist junk because they identify as someone healthy. The behavior flows from identity. (ref: [[2026-02-22]])

## The "Rules" That Block Change

Michael has internalized these rules (from life coaching work):
1. "My life isn't solid enough foundation for future" (insecurity)
2. "I must prove to family I'm living a great life" (external validation)
3. "I must prove I can juggle everything at professional level" (ego/achievement)
4. **Core Belief**: "Fun and joy are side-products, not real value" (ref: [[2026-02-22]] + life coaching session)

These rules create self-sabotage because they deny the reality of what's already working.

## All Behavior is Goal-Directed (ref: [[2026-02-22]])

**Key Reframe**: You don't "lack discipline." You're perfectly disciplined toward your _actual_ goals—which may be unconscious.

Example: You procrastinate on work not because you're lazy, but because you're excellently pursuing an unconscious goal: "Protect myself from criticism by not releasing my work."

Real change means changing the underlying goal, not just the surface behavior.

**Your Unconscious Goals** (patterns to notice):
- Protecting yourself from judgment
- Maintaining "safe, predictable" (even if mediocre)
- Keeping distance to avoid depending on others
- Building "respectable facade" to avoid shame

## The Path: From "Should" to "Want"

**Old Frame**: "I should want to become a dance therapist" (externally driven)

**New Frame**: "Do I genuinely want to be a dance therapist, or am I trying to make dance 'productive' to justify it?" (authenticity check)

**Distinction**: Being pulled toward something vs. being pushed away from perceived failure

**Your Realization** (from life coaching): The "pulled" goals are Iyengar/Gyrokinesis certifications (teaching feedback loop). The "pushed" goal was Master's degree (making joy productive).

## Uniqueness & Non-Perfection (ref: [[2016-12-16]])

**Wisdom Collected**:
- "I have a lot to offer. The unique _combination_ matters more than being the best in any one thing."
- "Don't need to be the best, but relatively good at something, and offer unique combination of skills" (ref: [[2016-12-16]])
- "People accept you not because you don't have flaws, but because you have unique strengths"

**Application**: Stop comparing to single-track experts. Your value is the DATA + DANCE + STRATEGIC + EMOTIONAL combination. That's uncommon and powerful.

## Business as Tool, Not Goal (ref: [[2016-12-16]])

"Business is a tool, a way to achieve the goal. If your goal is something higher than you, business can be very beneficial. If I feel ready or know the time, I will go. Don't rush into either/or decisions." (ref: [[2016-12-16]])

**Implication**: Entrepreneurship ideas (Movement Lab, content creation) are tools for expression, not destinations. Keep full-time job. Let them develop organically.

## Emotional Intelligence & Positive Self-Talk (ref: [[2016-12-16]])

- "The way you deliver things doesn't necessarily need to be harsh"
- "Start thinking positive about myself, not negative"
- "There is infinite knowledge; do the best you can, but don't overwhelm yourself"
- "Have your own practice. Do it from true heart, and it's ok."

**Action**: Replace critical inner voice with supportive one. You're already doing well; the issue is recognizing it.

## Procrastination: Neuroscience Foundation (ref: [[困扰我15年的拖延症，被一个反直觉的方法终结了！你的大脑在求救，希望你越早知道越好。|困扰我15年的拖延症]])

Chronic procrastination is not a willpower problem — it is a chronic stress response. Four evidence-based interventions, none relying on discipline:

- (new) **The cortisol trap**: 2019 *Psychological Science* study of ~10,000 participants found chronic procrastinators have elevated cortisol long-term. Procrastination isn't low pressure — it's too much pressure triggering the brain's escape mode. Every "just do it" technique adds more pressure to an already overloaded system.
- (new) **Reverse to-do list**: Write down everything you *did* complete (not what's left). Restores sense of agency, counteracts the overwhelm spiral. Counter-intuitive but clinically tested.
- (new) **Two-minute rule + 15-minute threshold**: The activation cost of starting is the real barrier, not the task itself. Commit to just 2 minutes. Once past the 15-minute mark, the nervous system disengages from threat mode and flow becomes possible.
- (new) **"Garbage version" method**: Perfectionism drives procrastination. The antidote: explicitly aim for the worst possible first draft. Removes the identity stake from the opening move. (Mirrors _POS "Ship Draft 1 immediately" patch for Competence Freeze.)
- (new) **Simulation game perspective (模拟人生视角)**: Detach from self-identity during task start. Imagine you are a character in a game with this task to complete — not *you* doing it. Strips the emotional weight from initiation.
- (new) **Diet–execution link**: The video cites specific nutrition research: previous day's food quality measurably affects next-day cortisol and executive function. Late-night high-sugar meals → worse task initiation next morning. Aligns with _POS 16+8 fasting protocol.

**Connection to _POS patterns**: The cortisol trap is the physiological mechanism behind the "Certainty Trap / Decision Paralysis" bug. The escape mode isn't laziness — it's the nervous system filing the task as a threat. All three manifestations (decision paralysis, think-want confusion, competence freeze) have the same substrate.

## Identity Courage: The 22-Year-Old Entrepreneur (ref: [[22岁的我，为什么辞去年薪150万的工作去创业？|22岁的我，为什么辞去年薪150万的工作去创业]])

A long-form reflection from Denny Liu (Wharton, '17) on desire, external validation, and the gap between what we think we want and what actually brings satisfaction. Key distilled insights:

- (new) **The vision-satisfaction gap**: We always evaluate future success using *today's* values and perspective. A 22-year-old wanting a Porsche imagines future-self feeling satisfied — but the perspective shifts as you grow. "You can't see yourself driving the Porsche." The goal that motivates you at 22 won't satisfy you at 35.
- (new) **Desire vs. programming**: Distinguish between what you genuinely want (internally generated) vs. what has been installed by family, society, comparison culture. Most people don't examine this. Clarity on this is prerequisite to authentic choice.
- (new) **"All or nothing" is a false binary**: The article models a third path — not "stay at the prestigious job" nor "quit and fail" — but a deliberate, resource-aware transition based on genuine desire rather than either fear or ego. Applies directly to portfolio career design: the "jump" doesn't have to be binary.
- (new) **Connection to _POS**: This maps to the "Think-Want Confusion" manifestation of the Certainty Trap. The article's framework for separating desire from programming is essentially a manual version of the "body as NO signal" patch.

## High-Function Survivor Pattern (ref: [[撐起一切的「高功能倖存者」：努力的背後是家庭創傷？ ft. 周慕姿 @muerstalk  ｜Sherry's Notes 雪力的心理學筆記|高功能倖存者]])

- (new) **Definition**: 高功能倖存者 ("high-function survivor") — people who appear capable, emotionally stable, and high-performing as adults, but whose competence originated as a survival strategy in an unstable childhood environment. Performance = safety exchange, not authentic identity.
- (new) **The core wound**: "If I am not useful, am I still worthy of love?" The internal economy conflates value with output. Rest, play, or non-performance feels dangerous — not because of external consequences, but because it triggers an old equation: low output = low worth = rejection.
- (new) **Role-locking in relationships**: In partnerships, a high-function person tends to lock into the "high-functioning" role (provider, problem-solver) while drawing in a partner who plays the "low-function" role (more dependent, less regulated). The asymmetry can look like complementarity but is actually a trauma configuration.
- (new) **Connection to current patterns**: The "Fun and joy are side-products" rule from POS 1.1 may have roots here — performance-as-identity is not just a belief, it's a structural response to earlier conditions. Worth exploring with Zhou Muzi's book: 《高功能倖存者：如果不「有用」，我還值得被愛嗎？》
