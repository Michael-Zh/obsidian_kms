# [[Life-Design-Methodology]]

## Core Framework (Stanford DYL)

Based on *Designing Your Life* by Burnett & Evans. Key shifts:

- **You Are Here**: Honest dashboard across all life dimensions before planning
- **Odyssey Plans**: Three parallel viable paths for the same life (not a single "right answer")
- **Prototype Experiments**: Low-cost, real-world tests that give data before committing — not planning, doing
- **Reframe gravity problems**: Problems that feel immovable often aren't; they're dysfunctional beliefs masquerading as facts

## The Three Odyssey Plans

**(new) Plan 1 — Continue Corporate Strategist Path**
- Ideal state: develop data intuition like manager; maintain 3 days/week body/beauty creation
- Work mix: ~50% pattern-finding, ~25% coaching others, ~25% execution
- Key insight: not chasing a copy of the manager's intuition — instead reverse-engineering her judgments into a structured knowledge base
- (ref: [[Life_Design_Coaching_Transcript]])

**(new) Plan 2 — Body & Movement Educator (The Realistic Pivot)**
- Systematic certification path: Gyrotonic, Iyengar, Pilates
- Teaching and coaching in Amsterdam movement community
- Enabled after financial security is established
- Bridge to Plan 3, not a lesser version of it
- (ref: [[Life_Design_Coaching_Transcript]])

**(new) Plan 3 — Somatic Experience Curator (The Wild Card)**
- Cross-disciplinary performing, exhibition curation, international travel
- Movement + aesthetics + culture as integrated practice
- Longer horizon — requires financial foundation + Plan 2 capabilities

## Key Reframes

**(new) Curation as high-level creativity**: Not generating from zero — the act of selection, synthesis, and growing-seeds-to-trees is rare, high-value creative work. The self-assessment "I'm not creative enough" is a misidentification of capability type.

**(new) 5% monthly investment rule**: Boring Investor applied to life design. Reserve 5% of monthly time for one new skill prototype. No need for urgency or large commitments. Compound interest applies.

**(new) Motivation reframe**: Don't wait for crisis or "dead end" to motivate change. Use the same Boring Investor patience strategy: small, consistent prototypes over time. The absence of crisis is not a reason to stall.
