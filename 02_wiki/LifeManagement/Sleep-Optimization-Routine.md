# [[Sleep-Optimization-Routine]]

## The Problem: Chaotic Pop-Up Mind at Bed

When lying in bed, mind becomes a "pop-up party":
- Dance piece ideas appear
- Work emails to send
- Strategy insights
- Worries and timelines
- Piano chords to learn
- New to-dos

This cascades into: can't settle → rehearse ideas all night to keep them safe → sleep is stolen (ref: [[coaching_insight_20251104]])

## The Solution: "Capture and Close" Principle

**Key Insight**: Don't fight the habit. Don't try to silence the mind. Instead, give it _structured time_ to empty and _trust_ that ideas are captured safe. (ref: [[coaching_insight_20251104]])

Once an idea is on paper, the brain's job is done. It no longer needs to _rehearse_ the idea all night to keep it safe.

## Brain Dump Ritual (Experiment)

**When**: 30 minutes before intended sleep (e.g., if sleeping at 2:00 AM, do brain dump at 1:30 AM)

**Setup**:
1. Set alarm called "Brain Dump"
2. Sit down (NOT in bed — important distinction)
3. Grab physical notebook and pen

**Process** (10-15 minutes):
- Write down EVERYTHING:
  - Ideas for dance piece
  - Email you need to send
  - Insight about strategy doc
  - Worry about timeline
  - Piano chord you want to learn
  - Random thought that popped up
  - Project idea
  - Reflection

**The Rule**: Once it's on the paper, your brain's job is done. It is _captured_. It no longer needs to rehearse the idea all night to keep it safe. It can trust that the paper will hold it until morning.

**Expected Outcome**: This structured action directly addresses the feeling of "I need to note this down now" by saying: "Yes, you do. Here is the time and place."

## Additional Sleep Optimization

- Sleep target: by 11:00 PM (15 min buffer to 12:00 PM) (ref: [[Habits-2026]])
- Avoid late-night dinner; plan meals carefully (ref: [[Habits-2026]])
- Experiment: milk before bed to help sleeping (ref: [[Habits-2026]])
- No phone in bedroom (better sleep hygiene and morning clarity) (ref: [[Habits-2026]])

## The "Night Owl" Reframe

"I'm a night owl" may not be identity—it's symptom of over-full day. By bedtime, mind finally gets uninterrupted space to come alive. (ref: [[Energy-Management]])

The goal is not to _fight_ the night owl nature or to force early sleep. The goal is to:
1. Acknowledge the excited mind as superpower (not problem)
2. Give it structured _time_ before bed
3. Allow body to rest after mind has safely emptied

## Activation Time

**Don't try for 10 PM bedtime tomorrow.** Small wins first:
- Start with brain dump ritual at 1:30 AM (or current late time)
- Aim for 1% improvement week-by-week
- Let the routine create its own momentum
