## Sleep Optimization: Brain Dump & Shutdown Routine

- **Root Issue**: Lying in bed with chaotic pop-up thoughts (ideas, to-dos, insights) — mind won't settle (ref: [[coaching_discussion_20251104]])
- **Solution**: "Capture and Close" principle — create structured "Brain Dump" before bed instead of fighting habit (ref: [[coaching_discussion_20251104]])
- **Brain Dump Ritual** (30 min before intended sleep):
  1. Set alarm for 30 min before sleep (e.g., 1:30 AM) called "Brain Dump"
  2. Sit down (NOT in bed) with physical notebook
  3. For 10-15 minutes, write everything: dance piece ideas, emails, strategy insights, worries, piano chords
  4. Rule: Once on paper, brain's job is done — idea is captured and safe (ref: [[coaching_discussion_20251104]])
- This addresses the "I need to note this down now" urgency by giving it structured time (ref: [[coaching_discussion_20251104]])

## Procrastination Neuroscience — Root Cause and Countermeasures (ref: [[困扰我15年的拖延症，被一个反直觉的方法终结了！你的大脑在求救，希望你越早知道越好。|困扰我15年的拖延症]])

(new) **Root cause is cortisol overload, not laziness**: Chronic procrastination correlates with elevated cortisol (stress hormone), not insufficient willpower. The brain's stress-protection mechanism activates avoidance. Trying harder = adding more load to an already overloaded system.

(new) **Cognitive paralysis mechanism**: Two conflicting internal directives running simultaneously ("I should work" + "I want to avoid") → brain CPU maxes out → freezes. Adding self-criticism opens a *third* process, making it worse. The "带着罪恶感浪费时间" (guilt-while-wasting-time) state is the worst possible — neither rest nor work.

(new) **Reverse to-do list**: Schedule your avoidance behavior explicitly — "3:00–3:30pm: browse X platform, minimum 20 posts, take it seriously." When the distraction becomes a *task*, the brain switches from hijacked mode back to in-control mode. Most people find they get bored after 10–15 minutes and choose to work instead.

(new) **Biological base before productivity hacks**: Sleep quality + morning dopamine management + nutrition are the hardware. No software (pomodoro, calendar blocking, willpower) runs well on broken hardware.
- Morning: No social media for the first 45 minutes (Huberman). High-dopamine spikes first thing raise the baseline so normal work feels boring.
- Sleep < 6 hours → prefrontal cortex (planning, impulse control, delayed gratification) significantly impairs. Not a willpower problem — hardware failure.
- Venn diagram rule: Just need 2 of the 3 circles overlapping (sleep + diet + exercise), with *sleep* mandatory in the 2.

(new) **2-minute rule + 15-minute threshold**: For anything that takes under 2 minutes — do it immediately; don't schedule. The value isn't the 2 minutes — it's the completion dopamine that resets the system toward action. The 15-minute timer: commit to just 15 minutes. Most tasks, once started, continue past the timer.

(new) **"Trash version" method**: Remove perfection gate from starting. First version is allowed to be trash. Perfectionism is an emotional attachment to the outcome — the Sims god-perspective (observe your character doing the task from above, no personal stake in quality) strips the emotion from the decision.

(new) **Connection to existing POS patterns**: The procrastination root cause (conflicting directives) is structurally identical to the KMS-known sleep issue — the Two-Track Protocol solves a similar problem by removing the conflicting directive ("I should fix this") from the active account. Same architecture: external rule fires before emotional pressure.
