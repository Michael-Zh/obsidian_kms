# [[Energy-Management]]

## The Activation Energy Paradox

You need energy to do the things that _create_ energy. It's the "activation energy" barrier—real and difficult to overcome.

**Problem**: Fighting both identity ("I'm a night owl") and powerful mental/physiological state ("my mind can't stop turning"). (ref: [[coaching_insight_20251104]])

## Energy Sources & Drains

### What Creates Energy
- Intensive exercise (gym, dance) — but followed by next-day energy crash (ref: [[Fitness-Routine]])
- Sleep optimization and proper recovery
- Morning routine (enables all other activities as solid foundation) (ref: [[coaching_insight_20251104]])
- Teaching/feedback loop (teaching motivates deeper practice; learning nurtures teaching) (ref: [[coaching_insight_20251104]])

### What Drains Energy
- Multiple intensive exercise days in succession (two classes = difficult next two days) (ref: [[Fitness-Routine]])
- Late nights due to excited mind and unprocessed thoughts (ref: [[Habits-2026]])
- Information overload without processing/actionability (ref: [[Information-Overload]])
- Lack of clear priorities; too many items competing for attention

## Night Owl Reframe

**Insight**: "Night owl" identity may not be a _fact_, but a _symptom_ of a life that's too full. (ref: [[coaching_insight_20251104]])

- By the time 11 PM hits, day's obligations are done and world is quiet — first chance brilliant mind gets to come out and play
- Brain "functions better" at night because it's the only uninterrupted free time
- **Question**: What if the solution isn't fighting the night owl, but giving the active mind structured time to empty?

## The "Excited Mind" as Superpower

**Core Reframe**: Do NOT try to "turn this off." The excited mind is the source of best ideas. (ref: [[coaching_insight_20251104]])

- Goal is NOT to silence mind
- Goal is to give it a _process_ so it can empty itself, trust ideas are safe, then allow body to rest
- Right now: lying in bed = "pop-up party" of ideas, to-dos, insights (chaotic)
- Solution: structured, intentional "party" _before_ bed (see [[Sleep-Optimization-Routine]])

## Time & Energy Budgeting

- Change reminder to priority-based with high/low load tags (but hard to decide load and don't actually sort by it) (ref: [[Habits-2026]])
- Too many items in list competing for energy; hard to decide what to do first
- Need to carve out 5 AM time for projects and admin amid training and main work
- Work content complexity requires constant pivoting between big direction, detailed analysis, and execution

## Environmental Design for Cognitive Performance (ref: [[细节决定成败：采访大咖的秘诀——极致体验与峰终定律]])

**(new) CO₂ concentration directly impairs cognition:** Research shows CO₂ above ~400 ppm equivalent cognitive impact to drinking one beer. In closed rooms, CO₂ rises quickly with occupants. Implication for sustained work or important conversations: ventilate regularly or work near open windows.

**(new) Peak-End Rule for designed experiences:** Kahneman's finding — people judge experiences by (1) the peak emotional moment and (2) the ending, not the average. Steven Bartlett's team uses this to craft extraordinary interview experiences: pre-researching the guest's first concert ever, controlling room scent, gifting a custom diary of highlights at the end. Result: guests remember the experience as exceptional even if parts were ordinary.

**Application to personal context:** 
- For important meetings or 1-on-1s with J/Vivi: what's the peak moment and the closing move? The analytical quality is table stakes; the emotional impression is what's remembered.
- For coaching/teaching in Plan 2 context: the exit experience (what students leave with) matters as much as the content delivered.
- For home environment: scent, air quality, and CO₂ management are cheap, high-ROI levers for sustained cognitive work.
