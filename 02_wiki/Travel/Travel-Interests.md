4. Review rating: ≥ 8.0/10 or ≥ 4.0/5
5. Surroundings: safe and conveniently located
6. Relatively new or recently renovated
7. Higher floor preferred

## Travel Philosophy — 围城 (The World as a Siege)

- (new) **Egypt reflection — the world as a "siege" (2026-05): from 《这世界是一所巨大的围城》**: Every country has its own inescapable tradeoff. Egypt: low cost + strong service culture, but physically draining (air, noise, food). Netherlands: terrible food, but deeply nourishing (calm, moist air, rhythm). The developed/developing divide doesn't map to "better" — it maps to *different problems*. (annotation: resonates strongly with personal experience across countries) (ref: [[2026-05-29_埃及]])
- (new) **"Nourishing" vs "satisfying"**: Places that maximally satisfy material wants are often not the places that let the nervous system rest. The places that truly nourish are those where the body and nervous system can relax — not necessarily the most comfortable or cheapest (ref: [[2026-05-29_埃及]])
- (new) **层级的恶 ("layered evil")**: Observation that crude villainy in poor areas is simpler and more legible. Sophisticated, organized harm is more dangerous — "packaged in respectability." Complexity of systems enables higher-cognition wrongdoing. Worth carrying as a lens for corporate and urban environments (ref: [[2026-05-29_埃及]])
- (new) **"Escaping" as an illusion**: Serial relocation and "second migration" (二润) just trades one set of problems for another. The real question is: which set of problems can you live with? Travel reveals this; it's not the answer itself (ref: [[2026-05-29_埃及]])
