# [[Portfolio-Career-Design]]

## The Core Dilemma

Tension between three truths:
1. **Corporate Reality**: Stable, financially efficient career as data analyst (current strength)
2. **Creative Passion**: Deep, pro-level practice in dance, yoga, movement (real joy)
3. **Identity Question**: Why does "fun" and "joy" not count as value?

**Key Insight**: Current life is objectively successful. You have both:
- Successful corporate career
- Pro-level dance life + certification teaching

But internal rules invalidate the joy part, pushing toward "productivity" justification.

## The "Rules" Creating Tension

1. **Insecurity Rule**: "My life now isn't solid enough foundation for future" (retirement, kids, family stability)
2. **Family/Validation Rule**: "Must prove to family I'm living a 'great life'"
3. **Ego Rule**: "Must prove I can juggle everything at professional level"
4. **Core Belief**: "Fun and joy are just 'side products' — they don't count as value"

These rules drive the search for Dance Therapy Master's as "perfect solution" to make dance "productive."

## Portfolio Career Model (3 Pillars)

Reframe from "either/or" to "and/also":

### Pillar 1: The Financial Engine (60-70%)
- Location-independent Data & Strategy role (either current role modified, or freelance/consulting)
- Provides stability, work-from-anywhere freedom, financial efficiency
- **Key Question**: How to make current data career location-independent?

### Pillar 2: The Passion & Teaching Loop (20-30%)
- Deep movement practice (Iyengar, Gyrokinesis, yoga, dance)
- Teaching practice (feedback loop: teaching motivates deeper practice; learning nurtures teaching)
- Not about "replacing" income; about fulfilling soul
- Can teach locally wherever you are, or explore online options

### Pillar 3: The Foundation
- Personal health, morning routine, physical practice
- Fuels the other two pillars

**Why This Works**: Honors both stability AND passion. Honors both corporate skill AND movement intelligence.

## Movement-as-Career Options

### High Priority: Certifications (not Master's)
- **Iyengar Certification**: Deep, personal dive feeding teaching practice
- **Gyrokinesis Certification**: Can often be done in modules (not all-or-nothing)
- **Advantage**: Unlike Master's, can integrate teaching with current work
- **Advantage**: Teaching can scale (local studio hours or online)
- Match your desire for "teaching feedback loop" perfectly

### Lower Priority: Dance Therapy Master's
- Logical "bridge" between dance and career, but check the driver:
  - Being **pulled** by genuine desire to be therapist? (Check day-to-day reality of dance therapy work)
  - Being **pushed** by need to make dance "productive" to justify it?
- Therapy is demanding emotionally/mentally. Is _that_ the calling you feel?

## Location-Independence Strategy

This is THE critical filter because it changes everything:

**If current data role IS or CAN BECOME flexible:**
- Golden ticket! Don't need to "replace" it
- It becomes stable pillar that _funds_ passion pillars
- Leverage the efficient money-making, add teaching/movement work

**If current role is NOT flexible:**
- Real top priority becomes: find location-independent data/strategy role
- Not a Dance Therapy Master's (that's the end game, not the path)

**(new) Updated framing (2026-05-30):** Location-independence is NOT being sought through the current role. The current role provides financial stability and resource security for the next 3 years. The question instead is: what parallel experiments and research can be done *alongside* the current role to prepare a future jump? This is a deliberate choice to use the corporate track as financial engine while building the parallel capability — consistent with "Business as Tool" in _POS.md_. The sequence shifts from "find location-independent role first" to "build location-independent capability in parallel." First concrete experiment still to be named. (ref: [[coaching_insight_20260529]], [[coaching_session_20260530_adhoc]])

## The "Justification" Question

**Deeper Question**: Where does need to justify dance come from?
- Internal insecurity?
- External pressure (family, society)?
- Belief that "fun," "joy," "passion" are less valuable than "utility" or "making money"?

**Reframe**: You've achieved a balance that most artists would give anything for. That's not failure. That's spectacular success.

## Next Steps

Two parallel tracks:

**Track 1: The Practical (Foundation)**
- Identify ONE tiny change this week to shift current patterns
- 1% improvement, not perfection

**Track 2: The Vision (Location-Independence)**
