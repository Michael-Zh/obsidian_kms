# [[Career-Confidence-and-Delivery]]

## The Core Question

What does confident delivery actually look like for me — and how do I know when I'm in it vs. performing around anxiety?

## Signals of Confident Delivery (emerging)

- **Analysis confidence**: Growing trust in own business analysis and strategic framing; cross-cultural edge identified as genuine differentiator (ref: [[Strategist-Requirements]])
- **Delivery rhythm**: Noticing the difference between producing from fear of judgment vs. producing from genuine engagement
- **Identity shift in progress**: Moving from "I must prove I can juggle everything at professional level" (ego rule) toward "I contribute real value through data + strategy + cross-cultural lens" (ref: [[Identity-Based-Life-Philosophy]])

## The Anxiety Side (what to watch for)

- Performance anxiety activated by unclear OKR contribution → leads to over-scoping, circular thinking, delayed delivery (ref: [[Work-Performance-Anxiety]], [[OKR-Contribution]])
- Unconscious goal: "Protect myself from criticism by not releasing work" — perfectionism as avoidance (ref: [[Identity-Based-Life-Philosophy]])
- External validation rule: "Must prove to manager/team I'm delivering at professional level" — exhausting and not sustainable

## What's Working

- Scoping clearly (what's in / out of OKR) reduces anxiety by giving a defensible frame
- Naming the strategic value of analysis before presenting — reduces second-guessing
- H1 performance review as a calibration point: what does manager actually see vs. what you fear they see?

## Open Questions

- What does "delivery confidence" look like when I'm not anxious? What are the physical / cognitive signals?
- Is the anxiety about delivery quality, or about visibility and being judged?
- After H1 review: what feedback pattern emerges? Use it to update this page.
